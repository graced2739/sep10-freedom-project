Past/Present Technologies:

Making Oil from Just about Anything:

From recent technology we are able to use any carbon-based waste like turkey guts, used tires, cans etc. to turn them into oil. To turn these waste into oil they just have to go through a process called thermo-depolymerization.Thermo depolymerization is a depolymerization that uses hydrous pyrolysis (when organic compounds are heated to high temperatures) to reduce complex organic compounds into oil. According to people that developed this process, a ton of turkey waste can make 600 pounds of petroleum which can be used to produce oil.

Plant Your Roof:

Plant your roof is where buildings have gardens on top of their roofs. These roofs help absorb heat, reduce carbon dioxide impact by taking up CO2 and giving off oxygen, absorbs stormwater and also reduces summer air conditioning usage. Having these garden roofs can lead to butterflies, songbirds and other organisms having a nice stable environment to live in.

Let Plants and Microbes Clean Up After Us:

Bioremediation is the introduction of microorganisms and other forms of life that can consume and break down environmental pollutants. One example of this is the cleanup of nitrates in contaminated water with the help of microbes. Another example is using plants to uptake arsenic( a solid chemical element that is used in wood preservatives, alloys, and semiconductors and is extremely toxic) from contaminated soil. One example of this plant is the Arabidopsis.
